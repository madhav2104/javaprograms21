SECTION 2 — BUSINESS REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT (BRD)


Business Objective
The primary business objective is to enable a unified, high-quality view of customer and system data across the enterprise, supporting advanced analytics, segmentation, and operational efficiency. By standardizing metadata management and eliminating redundancy, the organization seeks to reduce costs, improve data governance, and empower business teams with consistent, reliable information for decision-making.

Stakeholders
Key stakeholders include the data engineering and platform teams responsible for implementing and maintaining the new model, as well as business users such as the marketing team who require a unified customer view for segmentation and campaign management. Additional stakeholders include data governance and compliance officers, who must ensure that PII is handled appropriately, and system owners from CRM, ERP, and internal services, who will need to align their data integration points with the new canonical model. Collaboration across these groups is essential to ensure successful adoption and ongoing maintenance of the solution.

Use Cases
A marketing analyst needs to segment customers based on unified attributes such as email, phone, and system usage patterns. With the new model, the analyst can join transactional data with the Customer Master entity using the canonical customer_id, ensuring accurate and complete segmentation without encountering duplicate or inconsistent records.

A data engineer is tasked with optimizing storage and processing costs for the organization's data platform. By refactoring pipelines to reference centralized master entities rather than duplicating metadata in every event, the engineer reduces storage requirements and streamlines data processing, resulting in improved performance and lower operational costs.

Expected Outcomes
The expected outcomes include a significant reduction in data duplication across pipelines, improved schema consistency, and enhanced data governance and compliance. Business users will benefit from a unified, reliable view of customer and system data, enabling more effective analytics and segmentation. The organization will realize cost savings through optimized storage and processing, and will be better positioned to maintain compliance with PII handling requirements.

Success Metrics (KPIs)
Success will be measured by reductions in storage and data transfer costs, improvements in data quality and consistency (as evidenced by fewer schema conflicts and integration issues), and increased adoption of the canonical model across systems. Additional KPIs include the percentage of events referencing master entities, the elimination of PII from transactional tables, and positive feedback from business users regarding the usability and reliability of the unified data platform.

Business Value
By implementing a standardized, canonical data model for customer and system metadata, the organization will unlock significant business value. This includes lower operational costs, improved data quality and governance, and enhanced ability to support business analytics and segmentation. The unified model will enable faster, more accurate decision-making and provide a scalable foundation for future data initiatives, positioning the organization for continued growth and innovation.
